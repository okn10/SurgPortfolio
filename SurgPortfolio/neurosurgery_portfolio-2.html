Interview preparation

The interview is 90 minutes across 4 stations of 15 minutes each, held online via Qpercom VIDs. ST1 appointability threshold is 65% of marks. Each station has 5 min reading time before it begins.

🧠
Clinical station
Clinical reasoning, neurosurgical knowledge, patient management
▼

You'll be given a clinical scenario — likely a neurosurgical emergency, ward problem, or outpatient dilemma. The assessors want to see structured thinking, safety awareness, and appropriate escalation. As a student/FY applicant you are not expected to know neurosurgical technique — you are expected to know how to think clinically and communicate clearly.

Key topics to master: Raised ICP management, GCS assessment and trauma, subarachnoid haemorrhage, spinal cord compression, post-op neurosurgery complications, consent, capacity.

📋
Management station
NHS systems, teamwork, professional scenarios, leadership
▼

Scenarios involving team conflict, clinical governance, error reporting, breaking bad news, or managing competing priorities. Framework thinking is rewarded. Know the four pillars of medical ethics (autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice). Show awareness of consultant responsibilities and team dynamics.

📞
Telephone referrals station
Structured referrals, prioritisation, communication under pressure
▼

You'll receive a referral scenario and be assessed on how you take a history, prioritise urgency, communicate clearly, and manage the clinical situation. Use SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation). This station rewards composure and structured communication over clinical knowledge.

⭐
Motivation & understanding of specialty
Why neurosurgery, career plan, awareness of current challenges
▼

Your chance to stand out. Assessors want to see genuine, specific motivation — not "I find the brain fascinating." They want to know what you've done, what you've read, what your career vision is, and that you understand the realities of training. Your entrepreneurial angle and interest in neurotech is a genuine differentiator here if delivered confidently.
