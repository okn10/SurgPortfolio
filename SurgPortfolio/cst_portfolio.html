Strategy: The fastest route to indicator A is a first author original research publication. Interview preparation

The portfolio is now assessed at interview. Assessors review your uploads for 15 minutes before you join, then ask you questions on two domains of their choosing. They can ask about any domain, even ones with no evidence. Assessors award a score based on both the uploaded evidence and your answers.

What assessors look for — two-domain questioning
Operative experience
Be ready to describe a specific case in detail — what the procedure was, your role, what you learned. Have 2–3 memorable cases rehearsed. Know the anatomy. Be honest about your level of involvement — over-claiming is flagged as a probity concern.
QI / Audit
Walk through your entire project: what standard you audited, why it mattered, what data you found in cycle 1, what intervention you implemented, and what changed in cycle 2. Know the PDSA cycle. Know why QI matters in surgery.
Publications / presentations
Know your paper inside out — methods, findings, limitations. If presenting, know your results cold. Be ready to explain why the question was clinically important. If collaborative, know exactly what your contribution was.
Teaching
Describe a specific session: what you taught, how you identified the gap, how you designed it, what feedback you received and how you responded to it. Know the principles of adult learning — Kolb's cycle, Miller's pyramid.
